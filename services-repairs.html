Repair vs. Replacement: How to Decide

Most roofing issues fall into two categories. Here's how we help you choose the right path.

Choose Repair If:

  • Roof is under 15-20 years old
  • Damage is localized (one leak, storm damage)
  • Rest of roof is in good condition
  • You want the most affordable short-term fix

Choose Replacement If:

  • Roof is near or past its lifespan
  • Multiple leaks or widespread damage
  • Repeated repairs within the last 2 years
  • You want long-term performance and curb appeal

When Roof Replacement Makes Sense

Full replacement restores system integrity when repairs are no longer cost-effective.

Replacement Triggers

Signs You Need Replacement

  • Widespread leaks across multiple slopes
  • Aging roof near end-of-life
  • Repeated repairs with recurring failures
  • Deck or underlayment deterioration
Long-Term Value

Benefits + Workflow

  • Restore full weather protection and upgrade material quality
  • Reduce recurring repair costs and improve home value
  1. Inspect: Scope definition and replacement planning
  2. Prepare: Tear-off and deck evaluation
  3. Install: New waterproofing layers and selected material
  4. Finish: Final quality check and site cleanup
