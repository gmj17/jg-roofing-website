Hurricane-Ready Roofing

Most roofs fail before shingles fly off. Here’s how we think about it.

Water intrusion & edge failures

Category 1–2 hurricanes bring sustained winds of 74–110 mph. Most damage happens at edges, penetrations, and transitions where water finds its way in.

  • Flashing details: Roof-to-wall transitions, chimney boots, vent pipes—these are the first points of failure.
  • Underlayment integrity: A quality underlayment catches water that gets past shingles.
  • Edge sealing: Gutter, soffit, and fascia connections must be properly sealed.
  • Deck preparation: Decking must be secure and free of rot or damage.

Uplift risk becomes real

Category 3 hurricanes bring sustained winds of 111–129 mph. At this level, roof uplift becomes a real threat. Wind doesn't just blow over shingles—it pulls the entire roof system upward.

  • Fastener strength: Nails and fasteners must meet wind-uplift specifications. Incorrect spacing or shallow penetration means failure.
  • Decking attachment: The roof deck itself must be properly fastened to the structure below.
  • Penetrations & weak points: Vents, skylights, and valleys create stress points where uplift starts.
  • System redundancy: Multiple layers of protection (shingles, underlayment, flashing, fastening) prevent total failure if one fails.

System integrity under extreme load

Category 4–5 hurricanes bring sustained winds of 130+ mph. At this level, the entire roof system is under extreme stress. We prioritize redundancy and total system performance.

  • Premium underlayment: Synthetic or reinforced underlayment provides secondary water protection if shingles are lost.
  • Enhanced fastening: More fasteners, proper spacing, correct depth—every connection matters.
  • Flashing quality: Heavy-gauge metal, proper sealing, and secure attachment prevent catastrophic failures.
  • Ventilation strategy: Proper attic ventilation prevents pressure buildup that can lift roofs.
  • Deck & structure: Underlying structure must be sound. Rotted or damaged decking creates total-failure risk.

Frequently Asked Questions

Quick answers homeowners ask us most in South Florida.

How fast can you come out for an inspection?

Typical scheduling depends on demand and weather.

  • For active leaks, calling is the fastest way to get help.
  • We’ll confirm a time window and what to expect during the visit.
  • We can also schedule pre-storm readiness checks.

Do you help with storm/insurance documentation?

Yes — we focus on clear documentation.

  • We can provide photo documentation of roof conditions.
  • We explain findings in plain English.
  • We’ll outline repair vs. replacement options based on the roof’s condition.

How do I know if I need a repair or a full replacement?

It depends on damage type, age, and system condition.

  • Repairs make sense for isolated damage and healthy surrounding areas.
  • Replacement is recommended when failures are widespread or the roof system is near end-of-life.
  • We’ll show you the evidence (photos) and the reasoning.

What affects roofing price the most?

Roof size, pitch, materials, and existing conditions.

  • Roof size/pitch (labor + safety complexity)
  • Roof system choice (underlayment, flashing, ventilation)
  • Decking/moisture issues (hidden conditions found during teardown)
